# Misuse & Responsible Interpretation Guidelines  
**Corruptibility Index (CI)**  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Public Ethical Framework  
**Last Updated:** December 2025  

---

# 1. Overview

These guidelines define the **ethical, responsible, and permitted uses** of the Corruptibility Index (CI) conceptual framework.  
They exist to protect:

- public figures,  
- journalists,  
- researchers,  
- civic technologists,  
- and the public.

Misinterpretation or misuse of CI can cause real harm.  
This document establishes guardrails and clarifies what CI is — and is *not* — intended to do.

---

# 2. CI Is a Framework, Not a Judgment Engine

It is critical to state:

### **CI does *not* determine wrongdoing or guilt.**  
### **CI does *not* claim any individual is corrupt.**  
### **CI is *not* predictive or accusatory.**

The CI framework analyzes **risk conditions**, not people.  
It interprets patterns in public-record domains traditionally studied in civic research.

A CI signal is **not a verdict**.

---

# 3. Types of Misuse (Strictly Prohibited)

The following uses of the CI framework are explicitly considered **misuse**:

### ❌ 1. Using CI to accuse any person of corruption  
CI does not and cannot establish guilt or wrongdoing.

### ❌ 2. Using CI to conduct personal or political attacks  
The framework must never be used as a partisan weapon.

### ❌ 3. Presenting CI outputs as definitive, predictive, or factual judgments  
CI does not forecast future behavior, criminality, or intent.

### ❌ 4. Using CI to harass, defame, or target individuals  
The framework is designed for systemic transparency, not personal attacks.

### ❌ 5. Combining CI with non-public or private data  
CI is grounded exclusively in public, lawful information.

### ❌ 6. Treating a high CI signal as proof  
A risk signal is not evidence of misconduct.

### ❌ 7. Publishing “lists” of supposedly corrupt individuals  
This violates both the intent and ethical design of CI.

### ❌ 8. Using CI to bypass due process  
Legal systems, ethics boards, and investigative bodies remain the only arbiters of wrongdoing.

### ❌ 9. Cherry-picking metrics to support a predetermined narrative  
CI requires context and balance.

---

# 4. Permitted Uses (Responsible, Ethical)

The CI framework may be used **responsibly** for:

### ✓ Academic research  
As a structured conceptual model for studying systemic corruption-risk factors.

### ✓ Civic technology development  
For building context-rich transparency tools grounded in public data.

### ✓ Journalism  
For understanding patterns in public-record information (not assigning blame).

### ✓ Public education  
Explaining how different transparency domains interrelate.

### ✓ Institutional transparency analysis  
Examining systemic conditions, not individuals.

### ✓ Governance modeling  
Exploring structural signals in civic systems.

### ✓ Ethical public discourse  
Only when careful to avoid personal accusations.

---

# 5. Interpretation Principles (Must Always Be Followed)

To prevent misuse, any CI-inspired analysis must follow these principles:

### **Principle 1 — Context Matters**  
No single data point should be interpreted in isolation.

### **Principle 2 — Risk ≠ Guilt**  
A risk signal suggests a pattern worth contextualizing, not wrongdoing.

### **Principle 3 — Public-Only Data**  
CI frameworks rely solely on public information.

### **Principle 4 — Transparency Over Sensationalism**  
Avoid headlines, insinuations, or claims unsupported by evidence.

### **Principle 5 — Fairness and Nonpartisanship**  
CI is neutral and must be applied without political bias.

### **Principle 6 — Respect for Individuals**  
The dignity and rights of public figures must be upheld at all times.

### **Principle 7 — Method Disclosure**  
If CI-inspired analyses are used, methods must be clearly explained.

### **Principle 8 — Multi-Source Verification**  
CI signals should always be cross-checked with primary records.

# 6. Examples of Misinterpretation (Clarified)

The following examples show **incorrect vs. correct** interpretations:

### ❌ Incorrect:  
“CI says this person is corrupt.”

### ✓ Correct:  
“CI highlights public-record patterns traditionally associated with transparency risk in civic research.”

---

### ❌ Incorrect:  
“A high CI score proves misconduct.”

### ✓ Correct:  
“A high signal means several public-record domains show unusual patterns that should be interpreted cautiously and contextually.”

---

### ❌ Incorrect:  
“CI predicts future behavior.”

### ✓ Correct:  
“CI analyzes structural transparency factors; it is not predictive.”

---

### ❌ Incorrect:  
“CI is evidence that someone violated the law.”

### ✓ Correct:  
“CI is not a legal or ethical finding and should never be used as evidence.”

---

# 7. Zero-Tolerance Policy for Political Weaponization

CI forbids:

- partisan targeting,  
- attack ads,  
- electioneering,  
- fear-based messaging,  
- selective misuse of metric families.

Any use of CI to advance a political agenda is **explicitly disallowed**.

The framework is designed to serve **democracy**, not partisanship.

# 8. Protection of Public Figures

These guidelines exist to ensure:

- fairness  
- neutrality  
- transparency  
- ethical boundaries  
- respect for due process  
- protection from false implications  

CI treats public figures as **partners in transparency**, not adversaries.
